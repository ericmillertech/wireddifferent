Lesson 01 · Intro to Cybersecurity

What even is cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is about protecting systems, networks, and data from people who shouldn't have access to them. That's it. Everything else — firewalls, encryption, threat detection — is just a specific way of doing that job.

The reason it's a $188 billion industry is because there are millions of systems worth protecting and not nearly enough people who know how to protect them.

// PLAIN ENGLISH ANALOGY Think of every company's digital infrastructure like a building. Cybersecurity is the team responsible for the locks, the cameras, the alarm system, and knowing what to do when someone tries to break in.
Lesson 01 · Why Your Brain Is Built For This

// TRAITS THAT TRANSLATE Pattern recognition under pressure · Hyperfocus during incident response · Creative problem-solving when logic fails · High tolerance for ambiguity and fast-changing situations

The industry has a 3.4 million job shortage. It's not a talent shortage — it's a pipeline problem. The people who need this career most are the ones who were told they couldn't do it.

Lesson 01 · The First Real Concept

What is a firewall — actually?

A firewall is a filter that sits between a network and everything trying to connect to it. It checks every incoming and outgoing connection against a set of rules and decides: let it through, or block it.

// THE ANALOGY THAT ACTUALLY STICKS A firewall is a bouncer at the door of a venue. It has a guest list (the rules). It checks every person trying to get in. If you're on the list — you're in. If not — you're not getting past that rope.

That's the whole concept. Everything else about firewalls is just a variation of that idea. Once you have the mental model, the technical details fill in naturally.

Lesson 01 · Your Micro-Task

One thing to do in the next 25 minutes.

ADHD-friendly studying doesn't mean shorter. It means scoped to a finish line you can actually see. Here's yours:

// THIS SESSION'S MICRO-TASK

Open a blank note. Write down, in your own words: What is a firewall and what does it do? Aim for 3 sentences. Doesn't need to be perfect. Just prove to yourself the idea landed.

That's it. If you can explain it in plain English, you understand it better than most people who "studied" it for hours.
