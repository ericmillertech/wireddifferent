The Night I Almost Quit — And What I Did Instead

It was 11pm. I'd been staring at the same practice exam question for two hours. 23 browser tabs open. Notes scattered across three apps. I retained nothing.

And I genuinely thought: maybe this just isn't for me.

I didn't quit that night. But not because of willpower — I don't have unlimited willpower, and if you're reading this, you probably know that feeling. I found one reframe that actually worked. Not a hack. Not a productivity trick. A different way of thinking about what I was trying to do.
